## Working with Your Case

### Documents Tab üìÅ
**Organize your evidence and paperwork**

1. Tap **"+ Create Folder"** to make a new folder
2. Choose a category (Evidence, Court Documents, Correspondence, etc.)
3. Tap the folder to open it
4. Tap **"+ Add Document"** to:
   - Take a photo with your camera
   - Choose from photo library
   - Import PDFs and other files
5. All images are automatically converted to PDF

**Best Practice**: Organize documents by category to make them easy to find later.

### Timeline Tab üìÖ
**Build a chronological story of your case**

1. Tap **"+ Add Event"** to create a new timeline entry
2. Fill in:
   - **Title**: Brief description (e.g., "Car accident occurred")
   - **Date**: When it happened
   - **Description**: Details about the event
   - **Category**: Type of event (Incident, Filing, Hearing, etc.)
   - **Importance**: High, Medium, or Low
3. Events are automatically sorted by date
4. Filter by category using the buttons at the top

**Best Practice**: Add events as they happen so you don't forget important details.

### Witnesses Tab üë•
**Keep track of people who saw what happened**

1. Tap **"+ Add Witness"** to create a new witness record
2. Enter:
   - **Name** (required)
   - **Phone**, **Email**, **Address** (optional but helpful)
   - **Relationship**: How they're involved in the case
   - **Notes**: Any important information
3. Add statements by tapping **"Add Statement"**
4. Mark witness availability

**Best Practice**: Get witness contact information right away - it's hard to find people later!

### Expenses Tab üí∞
**Track every dollar you spend on your case**

1. Tap **"+ Add Expense"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Description**: What you paid for
   - **Amount**: How much it cost
   - **Category**: Type of expense
   - **Date**: When you paid
   - **Reimbursable**: Check if you might get this money back
3. View totals at the top:
   - Total spent
   - Reimbursable amount

**Best Practice**: Keep receipts! Take photos and save them as documents.

**Expense Categories**:
- Filing Fees
- Document Copies  
- Travel/Mileage
- Postage/Shipping
- Expert Fees
- Medical Records
- Notary Fees
- Process Service
- Other

### Deadlines Tab ‚è∞
**Never miss an important court date**

1. Tap **"+ Add Deadline"**
2. Enter:
   - **Title**: What needs to be done
   - **Date**: When it's due
   - **Type**: Filing, Hearing, Discovery, or Response
   - **Priority**: High, Medium, or Low
   - **Description**: Additional details
3. Deadlines are organized into:
   - **Overdue** (in red) - Act immediately!
   - **Upcoming** - Keep track
   - **Completed** - Done

**Best Practice**: Add deadlines as soon as you get them. Court deadlines are strict!

### Notes Tab üéô
**Record your thoughts and observations**

1. Tap the **microphone button** to start recording
2. Speak your notes
3. Tap **stop** when done
4. Add:
   - **Title**: What the note is about
   - **Transcription**: Type what you said (optional)
   - **Tags**: Keywords for searching (e.g., "witness", "accident")
   - **Related to**: Link to a case event

**Best Practice**: Record notes right after important events while details are fresh.

**Note**: Make sure to allow microphone access when prompted.

### Calculator Tab üßÆ
**Estimate potential settlement value**

1. Enter **Economic Damages**:
   - Medical Expenses: All medical bills
   - Lost Wages: Income you couldn't earn
   - Property Damage: Cost to fix/replace property
   - Other Costs: Any other out-of-pocket expenses

2. Estimate **Non-Economic Damages**:
   - Set a multiplier for pain & suffering (typically 1.5x - 5x)
   - Higher multipliers for more serious injuries

3. Review the calculation:
   - Total Economic Damages
   - Estimated Non-Economic Damages  
   - **Total Estimated Value**

**Important**: This is just an estimate! Every case is different. Consult with an attorney for a real evaluation.

## Tips for Success

### Document Organization
- Create folders BEFORE taking photos
- Use clear, descriptive folder names
- Keep related documents together
- Name documents clearly (not just "IMG_1234")

### Building Your Case Timeline
- Add events as they happen
- Include both positive and negative events
- Attach supporting documents to timeline events
- Mark important events as "High" importance

### Working with Witnesses
- Get contact info immediately
- Ask witnesses to write statements while memory is fresh
- Keep track of who saw what
- Note if witnesses are willing to testify

### Financial Tracking
- Save every receipt
- Photograph receipts and add to documents
- Track mileage to court/attorney (may be reimbursable)
- Include parking fees, tolls, etc.

### Court Deadlines
- Add deadlines immediately when you receive them
- Set reminders a few days before deadline
- Court deadlines are STRICT - don't miss them!
- If you'll miss a deadline, file a motion for extension ASAP

### Voice Notes
- Record right after important events
- Include date, time, and what happened
- Note who was present
- Describe your injuries or damages
- Keep notes factual, not emotional

## Privacy & Security

### Your Data is Stored Locally
- All data stays on your device
- Nothing is uploaded to the cloud (currently)
- Back up your phone regularly
- Don't delete the app without backing up your data

### Sharing Information
- Be careful when sharing case information
- Use the export features to create PDF summaries
- Only share with trusted people (your attorney, family)

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I use this app to represent myself in court?**
A: This app helps you organize your case, but it's not a substitute for legal advice. Consider consulting an attorney, especially for serious matters.

**Q: Is this app only for Virginia?**
A: The legal resources (courts, legal aid, templates) are Virginia-specific, but the case organization features work anywhere.

**Q: Can I back up my data?**
A: Currently, data is stored locally. Back up your phone regularly. Cloud backup/sync is planned for future updates.

**Q: Can I share my case with my attorney?**
A: You can export documents and summaries to share with your attorney. Direct case sharing is planned for future updates.

**Q: Are the settlement calculator results accurate?**
A: The calculator provides rough estimates only. Every case is unique. Consult an attorney for a real evaluation.

**Q: What if I need legal advice?**
A: Use the Legal Aid Finder to find free or low-cost legal help. This app provides information, not legal advice.
