# ellio-law Limitations & Scope

**Version**: 1.0.0  
**Last Updated**: January 2026  
**Status**: Production

---

## Purpose

This document explicitly defines what ellio-law does and does not do. This clarity is essential for:
- Setting accurate user expectations
- Maintaining legal and ethical boundaries
- Ensuring app store compliance
- Building trust through transparency

---

## What ellio-law IS

### 1. Legal Navigation Tool

ellio-law helps people understand and navigate legal processes.

**This means**:
- Explaining court structures
- Defining legal terms in plain language
- Describing typical procedural steps
- Showing common paths through the court system

**This does not mean**:
- Analyzing individual circumstances
- Recommending specific legal strategies
- Predicting case outcomes
- Advising on legal rights or obligations

### 2. Document Organization System

ellio-law helps people organize legal documents, evidence, and case materials.

**This means**:
- Creating folders and categories
- Storing photos and files
- Converting images to PDFs
- Tagging documents by type and date
- Building chronological timelines

**This does not mean**:
- Determining what documents are legally required
- Analyzing whether evidence is sufficient
- Recommending what to file or when
- Interpreting document content

### 3. Legal Resource Directory

ellio-law connects people with legal help resources.

**This means**:
- Listing legal aid organizations
- Providing court contact information
- Showing eligibility guidelines
- Linking to official court websites

**This does not mean**:
- Guaranteeing service availability
- Endorsing specific organizations
- Determining eligibility
- Providing the legal help itself

### 4. Educational Information Source

ellio-law provides general educational content about legal processes.

**This means**:
- Explaining what terms mean
- Describing how processes typically work
- Showing court jurisdictions and structures
- Offering procedural templates as examples

**This does not mean**:
- Giving advice about specific situations
- Recommending what users should do
- Guaranteeing information applies to a user's case
- Replacing consultation with a lawyer

---

## What ellio-law IS NOT

### 1. Not a Lawyer

ellio-law does not:
- Provide legal advice
- Represent clients
- Analyze case facts
- Recommend legal strategies
- Predict legal outcomes
- Interpret laws for specific situations

### 2. Not Legal Advice

Nothing in ellio-law constitutes legal advice, including:
- Explanations of legal concepts
- Document templates
- Procedural workflows
- Resource suggestions
- Automated calculations

**Legal advice requires**: Licensed attorney analyzing your specific facts and circumstances.

**ellio-law provides**: General educational information.

### 3. Not a Court Filing Service

ellio-law does not:
- File documents with courts
- Submit forms electronically
- Guarantee documents are correct
- Ensure deadlines are met
- Interface with court systems

**Users remain responsible for**: All court filings, deadlines, and compliance.

### 4. Not Comprehensive or Guaranteed

ellio-law information is:
- General, not specific to individual cases
- Subject to state and local variation
- Educational, not prescriptive
- Potentially incomplete or outdated

**Users should**: Always verify information with official court sources or legal counsel.

## State & Jurisdiction Limitations

### State Law Variations

ellio-law includes information for all 50 US states and federal courts.

**However**:
- Laws vary significantly between states
- Court procedures differ by jurisdiction
- Local rules may override general information
- Information may not reflect recent changes

**Users must**: Verify all information with their specific court and jurisdiction.

### Federal vs. State Courts

ellio-law provides general information about both federal and state court systems.

**Limitations**:
- Federal and state procedures differ substantially
- Jurisdiction questions are complex
- We do not determine which court is appropriate

**Users should**: Consult legal resources or counsel to determine proper venue.

### Local Court Rules

- Many courts have specific local rules
- Forms and procedures vary by county
- Filing requirements differ by court
- ellio-law cannot account for all local variations

**Users must**: Check with their specific court for local requirements.

### Information Currency

Legal information changes:
- Laws are amended
- Court rules are updated
- Contact information changes
- Resources appear and disappear

**ellio-law commitment**: Reasonable efforts to maintain current information.

**ellio-law limitation**: Cannot guarantee all information is current.

**User responsibility**: Verify information with official sources.

### Information Completeness

ellio-law provides:
- General overviews
- Common scenarios
- Typical procedures
- Representative examples

ellio-law does not provide:
- Exhaustive legal research
- Comprehensive case law
- Every possible scenario
- Jurisdiction-specific depth

### Information Sources

Information comes from:
- Public court websites
- Legal aid organizations
- Government resources
- General legal reference materials

Information is:
- Reviewed for accuracy
- Simplified for readability
- Generalized for broad applicability

Information is not:

- Verified by attorneys in every jurisdiction
- Updated in real-time
- Comprehensive of all variations

## When to Seek Legal Counsel

Users should consult a lawyer when:
- Facing criminal charges
- Unsure about legal rights or obligations
- Dealing with complex legal issues
- Opposing party has representation
- Significant money or property at stake
- Case involves children or custody
- Uncertain about proper procedures
- Need advice about specific situation

**Finding a lawyer**:
- Use ellio-law's Legal Aid directory
- Contact state/local bar associations
- Call 211 for referrals
- Check legal aid organization websites
